False positives: Non-linearities in your headphones or DAC can create intermodulation distortion, aliasing the high-frequency tone into audible lower frequencies. You may be detecting the distortion artifact, not the tone itself.

False negatives: Many headphones and speakers roll off well before 20 kHz. A "fail" may mean your equipment can't reproduce the frequency, not that you can't hear it.

For best results, use high-quality headphones with a known flat frequency response.
